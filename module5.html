Week 5: Sensors and Actuators

This module focuses on understanding how embedded systems interact with the physical world through sensors (inputs) and actuators (outputs).

Key Learnings:

  • Understanding electronic circuits and components: Reinforcement of basic circuit principles and common components relevant to sensor/actuator integration.
  • Introduction to sensors and actuators: Definition, types, and fundamental principles of various sensors (e.g., temperature, light) and actuators (e.g., LEDs, motors, buzzers).
  • Interfacing external LEDs, switches, and pushbuttons with RP2040: Practical methods for connecting and programming basic input (switches/pushbuttons) and output (LEDs) components with the Raspberry Pi Pico (RP2040).

Projects/Exercises:

Day 1: Understanding electronic circuits and components.
Day 2: Introduction to sensors and actuators.
Day 3: Interfacing external LEDs, switches, and pushbuttons with RP2040.

Throughout this module, TinkerCAD was utilized for simulating circuits involving LEDs and pushbuttons to visualize their behavior before implementation on physical hardware.
